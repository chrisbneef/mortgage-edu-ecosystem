Being *pre-qualified* for a number isn't the same as that number being comfortable to live with.

## Pre-qualified vs. affordable

A lender's maximum is based on ratios. Your real budget should account for the life you want to
live around the payment.

## Debt-to-income, briefly

Your <GlossaryTerm slug="dti">DTI</GlossaryTerm> compares monthly debt to gross monthly income.
Most programs look for a total DTI under roughly 43%, though it varies by loan type.

<Callout>
Remember the full payment is <GlossaryTerm slug="piti">PITI</GlossaryTerm>: principal, interest,
taxes, and insurance, not just principal and interest.
</Callout>

## A simple rule of thumb

Sketch your monthly take-home, subtract everything you already spend, and see what's genuinely
left for housing. That number, not the lender's ceiling, is your budget.
