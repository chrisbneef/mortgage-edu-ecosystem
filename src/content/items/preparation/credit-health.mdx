Your credit score is one of the first things a lender looks at. It shapes whether you qualify
and what interest rate you're offered.

## What a credit score is

A credit score is a number — typically a <GlossaryTerm slug="fico">FICO</GlossaryTerm> score
between 300 and 850 — that summarizes how reliably you've repaid debt.

<Callout type="tip">
Checking your own credit is a "soft pull" and does **not** lower your score. Check early and often.
</Callout>

## What moves your score

- **Payment history** — the single biggest factor. Pay on time, every time.
- **Credit utilization** — how much of your available credit you're using. Aim under 30%.
- **Length of history**, **credit mix**, and **new inquiries** round out the picture.

## Before you apply

Pay down balances, avoid opening new accounts, and dispute errors on your report. Small moves
months ahead of an application can meaningfully improve your rate.
