You got a "conditional approval" and your stomach dropped a little at the word conditional. Don't let it. This is good news with a short to-do list attached. It means the underwriter is ready to approve your loan once you hand over a few specific items.

Think of it like a teacher saying you passed, just turn in the last worksheet. The loan is on track. You're filling in blanks, not starting over.

## What a condition actually is

A condition is a request for one piece of evidence the underwriter needs to feel confident. They aren't trying to trip you up. They're checking that the picture you painted in your application holds together.

The common ones show up again and again:

- An updated pay stub or bank statement, because the older ones aged out
- A letter of explanation for a large deposit, or for a gap in your address history
- Proof of homeowners insurance for the new place
- A gift letter, if family is helping with the down payment
- Proof of where your down-payment money came from

Most of these you can pull together in an afternoon. A few might need a quick email to your employer or your insurance agent, so start those early.

## How to clear them fast

The whole game here is speed and precision. Respond the same day if you can, and send exactly what they asked for. Not a similar document, not last month's version, the specific thing on the list. Half the delays in underwriting come from a buyer sending the wrong file and the request bouncing back.

If a condition confuses you, ask your loan officer to translate it instead of guessing. One clear question saves a week of mismatched documents.

<Callout type="warning">While your conditions are open, freeze your finances. No new credit accounts, no moving large sums between banks, no job change. Any of those can reset the clock or reopen questions you already answered.</Callout>

## Getting to clear to close

Once every condition is satisfied, your file moves to "clear to close." That's the green light. The lender can now prepare your final documents and lock in your closing date.

There's a simple pattern worth remembering: the faster you answer, the faster you close. Underwriters work through a queue, and a file that responds quickly keeps its spot. A file that goes quiet drifts to the bottom while everyone waits on you.

So keep your phone close for the next couple of weeks. When a condition lands in your inbox, treat it like a small fire to put out today, not a chore for the weekend. Clear them quickly and the keys come quickly too.
