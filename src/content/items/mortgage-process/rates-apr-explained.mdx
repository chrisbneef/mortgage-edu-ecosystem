The interest rate gets all the attention, but <GlossaryTerm slug="apr">APR</GlossaryTerm> is the
truer cost comparison.

## Rate vs. APR

The **rate** sets your monthly principal-and-interest payment. The **APR** folds in most fees,
so a low rate with high fees can have a higher APR than it first appears.

## Fixed vs. adjustable

- **Fixed:** the rate never changes, so it's predictable for the life of the loan.
- **Adjustable (ARM):** a lower intro rate that can move later.

<Callout type="tip">
Paying "points" up front buys a lower rate. It pays off only if you keep the loan long enough to
recoup the cost.
</Callout>
