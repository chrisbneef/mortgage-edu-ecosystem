A pre-approval moves only as fast as your paperwork. Gather it ahead of time and you can get an answer the same day. Hunt for it piece by piece and you'll stretch a quick process into a frustrating week.

## The core documents almost everyone needs

Most lenders ask for the same starting set, no matter how you earn your money:

- Pay stubs from the last 30 days
- W-2 forms from the last 2 years
- Federal tax returns from the last 2 years
- Bank statements from the last 2 months
- A government photo ID
- A list of your monthly debt payments

You'll also give consent for the lender to pull your credit. That part is on them once you say yes, so there's nothing to prepare beyond agreeing to it.

## If you're self-employed or work 1099

Steady W-2 income is the simplest case. If you run your own business or work as a contractor, plan to show a little more.

Self-employed buyers should expect to add a profit-and-loss statement and business tax returns on top of the personal ones. Gig and 1099 workers should pull together their 1099 forms and be ready to point to the matching deposits in their bank statements. Lenders want to see that the money you report actually landed in your account.

None of this means you're harder to approve. It just means your income takes a few more pages to prove, so having those pages ready matters even more.

<Callout type="info">Pay stubs older than about 30 days and bank statements older than about 60 days usually get rejected as stale. Grab fresh copies right before you apply.</Callout>

## The two things that trip people up

First, timing. Documents go stale. A pay stub from two months ago or a bank statement from last quarter will likely bounce back with a request for something current. Pulling everything the same week you apply keeps you out of that loop.

Second, large deposits. Any deposit that isn't your normal paycheck can raise a question, because lenders need to know it isn't borrowed money sneaking in. A gift, a tax refund, the sale of a car: each one needs a short paper trail and a sentence explaining where it came from. Sort that out before they ask and you avoid an awkward delay.

Build one folder, digital or physical, with all of this inside. Keep it updated as new pay stubs and statements come in. When you finally sit down with a lender, you'll hand over a complete package instead of scrambling, and that single habit is often the difference between a same-day answer and days of back-and-forth.

<Disclaimer>Every lender and loan program is different. This list covers the common requirements, but yours may ask for additional documents.</Disclaimer>
