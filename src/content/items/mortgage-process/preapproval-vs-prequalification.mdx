These two words get used like they mean the same thing. They don't. And the gap between them can decide whether your offer gets taken seriously or quietly passed over.

## What prequalification actually is

A prequalification is a quick estimate. You tell a lender your income, your debts, and roughly what you have saved, and they hand back a number you might be able to borrow. Often there are no documents involved. Sometimes there's a soft credit check, sometimes none at all.

It's useful early on. If you're just starting to wonder what's possible, a prequalification gives you a ballpark in a few minutes. Treat it as a sketch, not a promise. Because the lender hasn't verified anything you said, the number can shift a lot once real paperwork shows up.

## What pre-approval is, and why it carries weight

A pre-approval is the real one. Here the lender verifies your income, checks your assets, and pulls your credit for real. At the end you get a letter stating a specific amount they're prepared to lend you.

That letter is what sellers and agents want to see. It tells them a lender already looked under the hood and is comfortable. In a competitive market, an offer without a pre-approval letter often gets skipped, because the seller has no reason to believe you can actually close.

<Callout type="tip">Get pre-approved before you start touring homes, not after you've found one. When the right house appears, you'll want to move the same day.</Callout>

A pre-approval is usually good for 60 to 90 days. After that, your financial picture may have changed, so the lender will want fresh documents and another look. Keeping it current is just a matter of sending updated pay stubs and statements when they ask.

## A pre-approval is strong, but it's not the finish line

Here's the honest part. Even a pre-approval is not a final guarantee that your loan will fund. The home itself still has to clear underwriting, and the appraisal has to support the price you agreed to pay.

So think of it this way. Prequalification answers "roughly, could I?" Pre-approval answers "yes, this lender is ready, pending the house checking out." That second answer is the one that gets you to the closing table.

If you only do one thing before you start shopping, make it the pre-approval. It costs you a little paperwork now and saves you from falling for a home you can't actually offer on.

<Disclaimer>A pre-approval letter is not a loan commitment. Final approval depends on full underwriting, the property, and the appraisal.</Disclaimer>
