# Homebuyer Search-Intent Map — by Lifecycle Phase

A content-planning map: **phase → borrower mindset → search-intent clusters → real queries → content to build.** Use it to make sure each of the four phase views covers the questions people actually search. Every item is cross-tagged to the 5 content categories so it can be authored with the right `journeyCategory` / `phases` frontmatter.

**Categories:** (1) Preparation & Financial Foundation · (2) The Mortgage Process · (3) The Property Search · (4) Closing & Beyond · (5) Tools & Resources.

> **Provenance:** Built from mortgage/real-estate domain knowledge and **spot-validated against live search (June 2026)** on the highest-value clusters — affordability (28/36 rule), pre-approval docs + DTI (~43%), offer-to-close timeline (30–60 days), low-appraisal options (reconsideration of value), and PMI removal (80%/78% LTV). Validate the remaining long-tail query strings against a keyword tool before building at scale. Sources at the bottom.

## PHASE 1 — THE DREAM (Before Pre-Approval / "BPA")

**Mindset:** Emotionally invested in *the home and the lifestyle*, not the loan. Testing feasibility ("can I even do this?"), comparing rent vs. own, exploring places, prices, and budgets. Aspirational but anxious about affordability; doesn't yet self-identify as a "loan applicant." This is the high-volume top-of-funnel battleground owned by Zillow/Redfin/Rocket calculators.

## PHASE 2 — GETTING READY (Pre-Approval)

**Mindset:** The dream has hardened into a plan; they want to know exactly what they qualify for and how to make a lender say yes. Transactional and slightly anxious — they fear rejection, surprises, and looking unprepared. Four questions dominate: **monthly payment, do I qualify, can I get the letter, how much cash to close.**

Items 9–14 protect deals already in the funnel (the docs-on-time pain point). Items 15–18 build the long-tail re-engagement/refi moat. Calculators rank high because they double as lead-capture tools and compete head-to-head with Zillow/Rocket.
