## Language rules

**Always**
- State assumptions behind any number ("assuming a 700 credit score and 20% down…").
- Use "estimated," "example," or "illustrative" for any payment, rate, or APR figure.
- Distinguish **interest rate** from **APR** whenever either appears.
- Present pros and cons of any loan program neutrally.

**Never**
- Quote a specific live rate as if guaranteed, or imply a rate/approval is locked.
- Use "pre-approved," "guaranteed," "best," "lowest," or "no risk" as factual claims.
- Promise outcomes ("you will save," "you'll qualify") — use conditional language.
- Use urgency/pressure tactics ("act now," "limited time") — this is education, not advertising.
- Give individualized financial, tax, or legal advice — direct readers to a professional.

## Special topics

- **Trigger terms / advertising rules:** if a piece mentions a specific rate, payment amount, term,
  or "no down payment," additional Truth-in-Lending/Reg Z advertising disclosures may apply — flag
  for compliance review.
- **Government programs (FHA/VA/USDA):** state that the company is not affiliated with or endorsed
  by any government agency; programs have eligibility requirements.
- **State-specific:** maintain a per-state disclosure matrix as a separate `.md` here if needed.

# Mortgage Marketing Compliance Guidelines

*A general educational reference summarizing common compliance requirements for mortgage marketing and advertising. This material is provided for training purposes only and is not legal advice. Requirements vary by company, state, and regulator — always confirm current obligations with your company's compliance and legal teams before publishing.*

> **General rule:** All marketing and advertising collateral should be reviewed and approved by your company's marketing and compliance department before it is published or distributed.

## Marketing and Advertising Approval

All marketing and advertising collateral — including but not limited to brochures, flyers, advertisements, radio and video scripts, presentations, yard signs, web banners, social media content, emails, videos, giveaways, and event materials — should be submitted to the company's marketing and compliance department for review and approval prior to publication or distribution.

This approval step protects both the company and the consumer by ensuring required disclosures are present, claims are substantiated, and no language is misleading or prohibited.

---

## Marketing Compliance Standards

### Lender Name Standards

The company's full legal name should be used upon first mention in all advertising. The company name and logo should always be the most prominent element on any piece of marketing collateral or advertisement. Advertising must never misrepresent who the lender is, and must never give the impression that an individual loan officer or branch is the lender rather than the licensed company.

### Team Logos and Names

All team name and logo requests should be submitted to marketing and compliance for formal review and approval. Note that some team logos or names may require registration on the NMLS site.

### Guarantees or Promises to Meet Closing Deadlines

A guarantee is any promise or commitment made to a borrower that implies a specific result will be delivered — such as meeting a deadline, offering a financial benefit, or providing a service outcome.

- *Example: "Close in 21 days or we'll credit $1,000 toward closing costs."*

Any closing guarantee should clearly disclose all of the following:

- **Start date of the guarantee** — when the timeline begins (e.g., upon receipt of all required borrower documentation).
- **Borrower obligations** — what the borrower must do to maintain eligibility (e.g., submit documents promptly, respond to requests).
- **Eligibility limitations** — any excluded loan types (e.g., VA, bond, and brokered loans may not be eligible).
- **Standard borrower costs** — the borrower remains responsible for their down payment and any other standard costs.
- **Redeemable cash restrictions** — the guarantee is for lender credit only and cannot be redeemed for cash.
- **Incentive amount** — the value of the credit or benefit being offered (e.g., "$1,000 lender credit toward closing costs").
- **Closing requirements** — who the loan must close with and in what state (e.g., a specific loan officer or branch, in a specified state).
- **Promotion expiration date** — a specific end date for the promotion or guarantee.
- **Exclusions for external delays** — the guarantee does not apply to delays outside the lender's control, such as borrower inaction or third-party issues.

All guarantees should be reviewed and approved by marketing and compliance before use.

### Sweepstakes, Contests, and Raffles

All giveaways, sweepstakes, contests, and raffles should include a disclosure covering all of the following:

- No purchase necessary to enter or win (stated prominently).
- Entry period: start date/time and end date/time.
- A complete explanation of how a person can enter, including any online and offline methods.
- If entry requires a purchase or other payment, a free alternative method of entry that requires no payment or purchase.
- The number of times each person or household can enter, and the odds of winning.
- How winners will be selected and notified.
- The winner's response-time requirement and what happens if the winner does not respond.
- The estimated value of the prize.
- Taxes (if applicable): clarify that winners are responsible for all taxes. A W-9 and 1099 should be issued for prizes valued at $600 or more. Some states also have registration requirements depending on the total retail value of all prizes (for example, $5,000 in Florida and New York).
- Publicity consent: the company's ability to use the winner's likeness for advertising and promotional purposes without additional compensation (unless prohibited by law).
- General conditions: the company's right to cancel, suspend, or modify the promotion.
- A list of excluded states or locations (if applicable).
- The sponsor's name and address.
- If posted on social media, a statement that the promotion is not sponsored by the platform (Facebook, Instagram, etc.).

Your compliance department can provide the correct legal disclaimer for any promotion.

### RESPA Guidelines for Giveaways, Closing Gifts, and Gift Cards

Under RESPA (the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act), giveaways and gift cards can raise significant compliance concerns — particularly if they are used to solicit or reward referrals for settlement services (loans, title services, appraisals, etc.).

Loan officers should not offer anything that could be perceived as a "thing of value" in exchange for referrals.

Gift amounts and giveaways should be reviewed and approved by the appropriate internal departments (accounting, marketing, and compliance) before use.

**Generally permissible under RESPA:**

- **No expectation of referrals** — the giveaway is not tied to or conditioned on referrals of business.
- **Open to the general public** — the promotion is available to a broad audience, not just real estate agents, builders, or others who might refer business.
- **Promotional in nature** — the giveaway is clearly for brand marketing or consumer awareness, not a reward for sending business.
- **Of nominal value (if targeted)** — items given to individuals in a business relationship (e.g., REALTORS®) should be normal promotional materials (pens, mugs, etc.) or of nominal value (generally under $25), and not based on business generated.

The IRS recommends an annual limit of $25 for gifts per loan closed. Any closing gifts above that amount should be reviewed and approved internally; a reasonable cap (generally under $100) is recommended where higher-value gifts are permitted.

### Desk Rentals, MSAs, Lead Generation, and Co-Marketing

Lead generation and co-marketing initiatives with REALTORS® should all be reviewed and approved by marketing and compliance.

When co-marketing with REALTORS®, never explicitly state or imply an exclusive relationship between the company and the REALTOR®. Terms like "preferred lender" and "partnership" should not be used.

If a loan officer wants to occupy space in a REALTOR'S® office for any length of time, an official desk rental agreement should be drafted and reviewed by the company's legal department.

### Reverse Mortgage Ads

All reverse mortgage advertising should be reviewed and approved by compliance and marketing before use.

### Negative Reviews and Customer Complaints

Negative reviews and complaints should be reported to the appropriate internal team (management and/or compliance) immediately. Loan officers should not attempt to resolve negative reviews on their own. All negative reviews and complaints should be tracked for auditing purposes.

### Items Requiring Compliance Review

Submit the following for review and approval prior to use or distribution:

- Anything related to REALTORS® or other settlement-service providers, including co-branded marketing materials.

Product or loan-related content such as:

- Down payment assistance.
- "Zero down" programs.
- Rates or loan scenarios.
- Open house flyers.
- Trigger terms (e.g., number of payments, loan term, payment amounts, or any finance charge).

Alcohol-related advertising of any kind, including:

- Happy hours.
- Wine tastings.
- Images of alcohol.
- Bottle gifts or alcohol-themed closing gifts.

Giveaways, events, and presentations.

All advertising (digital or print), especially when it involves:

- Loan terms or borrower benefits.
- Third-party businesses.
- Contests or sweepstakes.
- Promised incentives of any kind.

Foreign-language advertising should also be reviewed, as disclosures and required language must be accurate in every language used.

### CE Credit Courses

If a lender offers REALTORS® a continuing-education (CE) credit course, the lender should charge for it — even a nominal amount — because covering the cost of CE credits (which REALTORS® are required to obtain) can be a direct RESPA violation.

All CE credit courses should be reviewed and approved by compliance and marketing before being offered.

### Website URLs

Some states have specific URL requirements. For example, Georgia and Washington require the loan officer's name (first or last) to appear in the URL itself.

URLs should not use terms like "lowest" or "best" rates, or include any language that implies unprovable claims or exaggerated comparisons.

Public-facing website URLs that promote mortgage products and generate leads or loan inquiries must be registered on the NMLS site. After registration, the website should be reviewed and approved by compliance and marketing before going live.

### Products and Programs

Advertisements that reference specific product or program names should be sent to compliance for review.

## Creating Your Own Marketing

Materials not created by the company's marketing team should be submitted to marketing and compliance for approval. Including the required elements below can help speed up approval turnaround:

- Company logo (or an approved team logo).
- Loan officer's name — must match the name listed on the NMLS Consumer Access website. If a loan officer advertises under a variation (e.g., omitting a middle name), the alternate name should be added to their NMLS record so they can be found under that version. This does not change their official name.
- Loan officer's approved title.
- Loan officer's NMLS ID.
- Company NMLS ID (placed next to the loan officer's NMLS ID).
- Licensed branch address.
- Loan officer phone number(s), at least one of which should match what is listed on the NMLS site.

**Required disclosure (template):**

> Copyright ©[Year] [Company Legal Name], NMLS #[Company NMLS ID]
> For complete licensing information, visit https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org/
> **Not a commitment to lend. All loans are subject to credit and underwriting approval. Additional terms and conditions apply. Not all applicants will qualify.** Loan approvals, products, and interest rates may vary and are subject to change without notice. [Company Legal Name] is not endorsed by, or acting on behalf of, HUD, FHA, USDA, VA, or any agency of the federal government. Corporate Address: [Corporate Address]. Toll-Free: [Toll-Free Number]. Equal Housing Lender.

Additional required elements:

- Equal Housing Lender logo. (The logo is preferred. If there is no room for it, the phrase "Equal Housing Lender" typed at the end of the disclosure may suffice, but HUD prefers the logo itself.)
- State-specific verbiage, where applicable. Many states require specific disclosure language and/or the company's state license number. Examples of state requirements include:
  - Arizona: state banker license number.
  - California: "Licensed by the Department of Financial Protection and Innovation under the California Residential Mortgage Lending Act."
  - Colorado: "Regulated by the Colorado Division of Real Estate."
  - Kansas: licensed mortgage company, with license number.
  - Massachusetts: mortgage broker and lender license number.
  - Mississippi: "Licensed by the Mississippi Department of Banking and Consumer Finance."
  - Montana: requires a direct link to the loan officer's NMLS website.
  - New Hampshire: "Licensed by the New Hampshire Banking Department."
  - Nevada: licensed Nevada mortgage lender, with license number.
  - New Jersey: "Licensed by the NJ Department of Banking and Insurance."
  - Ohio: state registration/license number.
  - Oregon: "Licensed under the Oregon Consumer Finance Act," with license number.
  - Rhode Island: "Rhode Island Licensed Lender."
  - Washington: Consumer Loan license number.
  - Virginia: company legal name and NMLS ID, with a reference to www.nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

Always confirm the exact wording and current license numbers for each state with your company's compliance department.

### Business Cards

A compliant business card generally includes:

1. Company logo.
2. Company NMLS ID (next to the loan officer's NMLS ID).
3. Loan officer's name as it appears on the NMLS site.
4. Loan officer's approved title.
5. Loan officer's NMLS ID.
6. Loan officer's direct phone number tied to the NMLS site (this is especially important in states such as Nevada).
7. Loan officer's branch address tied to the NMLS site.
8. If an email is included, a company email address or an approved vanity address.
9. Equal Housing Lender logo or wording.
10. State disclaimers, where applicable.

**Sample layout:**

> [Company Legal Name] • NMLS #[Company NMLS ID] • [State License # if required]
> First and Last Name
> Title
> NMLS #[LO NMLS ID]
> O: (000) 000-0000
> M: (000) 000-0000
> E: [email address]
> [Branch Address]
> [Website]

## Social Media Compliance

### Overview

Social media is an effective way to generate mortgage business and to interact with consumers, increase brand awareness, advertise products, and reach new audiences. Because social media is considered mortgage advertising, companies must manage the associated risks to the organization and to consumers through appropriate written procedures and controls.

Depending on the profile type, social media profiles should cover the following:

- Loan officer's name as registered with NMLS.
- Employee title.
- Loan officer NMLS ID.
- Company NMLS ID (next to the loan officer's NMLS ID).
- Contact information as registered with NMLS.
- Branch address.
- Company name.
- Equal Housing Lender logo or phrase.
- Licensed-states list (e.g., "Licensed Lending Area: MO, IL, CA, and TX").
- Link to an approved company-branded website or application page.
- Link to the company's licensing/disclosures page.
- Privacy policy, where required.
- Loan officer state-specific disclosures.

**Promotional items:**

- If a licensed team member is listed, their NMLS ID must be included.
- If a branch or team name is listed, the company NMLS ID must be added.
- If a phone number is listed, the company NMLS ID must be added.

### Requirements for Social Media Profiles

- Loan officer's name as it appears in NMLS, with NMLS number.
- Company name and NMLS ID.
- Branch address (or corporate address, if applicable).
- NMLS Consumer Access link: https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org/ (the link alone is sufficient).
- Equal Housing Opportunity / Lender designation.
- Link to the company's state disclosures page.

**Team logo standards:** The company logo should always be the more prominent logo, positioned first and to the left of any team logo. When listing a team, include the branch NMLS ID.

### Social Media Disclosures

- **Social media ads:** The required disclosure statement or hyperlink can be included in the description section, but not in the comment section.
- **Social media posts:** The required disclosure statement or hyperlink can be included in the graphic or the description section, but not in the comment section.

All corporate posts must include the corporate disclosure. All loan-officer posts must meet that loan officer's state-specific disclosure and advertising requirements. Consult compliance for state-specific guidance.

### Requirements by Post Type

**Basic image (posts and background/cover photos):**

- Company logo.
- Equal Housing Lender logo.
- Company NMLS ID.
- Appropriate state disclosure and https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

**Image with product information:**

- Company logo.
- Equal Housing Lender logo.
- Company NMLS ID.
- Correct product disclaimer (varies by product).
- Appropriate state disclosure and https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

**Image with ranking or award information:**

- Company logo.
- Equal Housing Lender logo.
- Company NMLS ID.
- Appropriate state disclosure and https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

**Image with internal volume information:**

- Company logo.
- Equal Housing Lender logo.
- Company NMLS ID.
- Appropriate state disclosure and https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org.

If a loan officer's name appears on any social media image, also include:

- Loan officer's name.
- Loan officer's NMLS ID.

### Exceptions

**Holiday / awareness posts:**

- Company NMLS ID not required.
- Loan officer NMLS ID not required if no job title is included.
- Equal Housing Lender logo not required.
- Corporate or loan-officer state-specific disclosure not required.

**Anniversary / birthday shout-outs:**

- Loan officer NMLS ID required.
- Equal Housing Lender logo required.
- Corporate disclosure not required.

**Charitable giving WITH rate/ad information or a request for customer engagement:**

- Company NMLS ID required.
- Equal Housing Lender logo required.
- Corporate disclosure required.

**Charitable giving WITHOUT rate/ad information or a request for customer engagement:**

- Company NMLS ID required.
- Equal Housing Lender logo required.
- Corporate disclosure not required.

**Employee recognition for activities or personal achievements:**

- Company NMLS ID not required.
- Loan officer NMLS ID not required if no job title is included.
- Equal Housing Lender logo not required.
- Loan-officer state-specific disclosures not required.

**Company loan-volume shout-outs (company-branded):**

- Company NMLS ID; if branch-specific, the branch NMLS ID.
- Equal Housing Lender logo required.
- Corporate disclosure required; if branch-specific, branch-specific disclosure.
- Volume month/year, with a disclosure such as: *Based on the company's internal production data for MONTH YEAR.*

**Loan-volume shout-outs (loan-officer-specific):**

- Company NMLS ID.
- Loan officer NMLS ID.
- Equal Housing Lender logo required.
- State-specific licensing disclosures required.
- Volume month/year, with a disclosure such as: *Based on the company's internal production data for MONTH YEAR.*

**Recruiting only:**

- Company NMLS ID.
- Equal Employment Opportunity disclosure required.
- Equal Housing Lender logo not required.
- Corporate disclosure statement not required.

> We are an Equal Opportunity Employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status, or any other status protected by law. This is a recruitment ad, not a solicitation for mortgage services. Loan officer roles require an NMLS license.

### What Not to Post

- To post specific products, request the required disclosures from compliance first.
- All mentions of interest rates must be accompanied by "APR" or "Annual Percentage Rate," and trigger terms must be covered.
- Never use government logos to promote a product.
- Never post special rates or promise rates.
- Obtain written permission from the buyer before posting closing photos.
- Never use profanity or offensive language.
- Avoid trigger terms and superlatives such as "rate," "credit," "best," "perfect," "specialist," etc.
- Never post videos that have not been reviewed and approved by compliance.
- Keep pages professional: no references to or images of drugs, alcohol, sex, politics, religion, private company information, or personal opinions about the company, coworkers, or social movements.

## Video Compliance

**Topics that should NOT be discussed on video:**

- Specific rates being offered. (Noting that rates are generally rising can be acceptable if it is factual and references a reputable source, but it should not be opinion-based or create a false sense of urgency.)
- Any specific finance charge amount.
- Any specific monthly payment amount.
- The number of payments or period of repayment.
- Financial-planning or tax-saving advice.
- Credit-improvement advice.
- Claims that the company is the "fastest," "easiest," or offers the "lowest" or "best" rates.
- Open house advertisements for REALTORS®.
- Servicing-related, post-close mortgage payment, or MI-removal advice.
- "Cash needed to close" amounts.
- Copyrighted images, scenes, or songs. (When in doubt, check with marketing.)
- Accepting bitcoin or marijuana as income or assets.

**Topics that may be mentioned only with the proper disclaimers** — either spoken in the video or placed clearly and conspicuously at the end of the video, in the caption, and/or at the bottom of the email. Request the appropriate disclaimer from compliance:

- Pre-qualifications, pre-approvals, and similar programs.
- VA, FHA, and USDA loans.
- Down payment assistance programs or niche products.
- Reverse mortgage loans.
- Builder or REALTOR® incentives.
- Charity-of-choice programs or donations.
- Contests, raffles, sweepstakes, and giveaways.
- Guarantees of any kind (e.g., 10-day or 21-day closings).
- Earnest money refunds.
- Affinity programs (incentives for employees of certain companies).
- Gifted funds.

**Required for all videos:** Submitting the video script to compliance before filming helps ensure compliance from the start, avoids the need to re-film, and can speed up approval.

The following should appear at the end of the video and/or at the bottom of the email (present in at least one place per video email):

- Company logo.
- Company NMLS ID (next to the loan officer's NMLS ID).
- Loan officer's name as registered with NMLS.
- Loan officer's NMLS ID.
- Loan officer's approved title.
- Equal Housing Lender logo.
- Loan officer's state-specific disclosure and advertising requirements.
- Branch ID (if applicable).
- A phone number registered on NMLS, if the loan officer says "contact me."
- Branch address as it appears on the NMLS Consumer Access website (if applicable).

**Example:**

> Full Name
> Title
> 000-000-0000
> [email address]
> LO NMLS # | Company NMLS #
> Disclosure
> Equal Housing Lender logo

## Advertising Compliance

### Promotional Items

- The company logo should appear on all promotional items.
- If a licensed team member is listed, their NMLS ID must be included.
- If a phone number is listed, the company NMLS ID must be added.

### Interview Forms

Interview forms are acceptable as long as they do not collect the six pieces of information that together trigger a mortgage loan application. When speaking with a client, it is best practice to enter the client's information into a prospect loan file in the loan origination system (LOS) in real time, since that is the system of record.

A loan officer can obtain verbal borrower authorization for a credit pull and note it in the prospect file. Nothing should be sent to a borrower to complete unless it is an online application or the official online application (the Uniform Residential Loan Application, Form 1003). The reason: application information held only on paper, outside the system of record, can create a TRID concern by appearing to avoid the application trigger. Keeping all of it in the LOS avoids that problem.

**The six pieces of data that trigger an application:**

1. The consumer's name.
2. The consumer's income.
3. The consumer's Social Security number (to obtain a credit report).
4. The property address.
5. An estimate of the property's value.
6. The mortgage loan amount sought.

### Testimonials

**Written consent — a signed testimonial release form — is required before using any borrower testimonial or review in advertising.**

- Obtain a signed release before using any client review in marketing materials, regardless of where the review was originally posted.
- Testimonials must be current. Reviews older than two years may be used only if the original source and year are clearly noted (e.g., "Client review from 2022, Google Reviews").
- Remove any mention of previous companies, brokerages, or lenders from a testimonial before use.
- Testimonials should not include specific rates, loan terms, or unique borrower circumstances.
- Avoid content implying the loan officer went to extraordinary lengths, since similar experiences cannot be guaranteed for all clients.
- Reviews posted on public platforms (e.g., Yelp, Facebook) may not be reproduced in marketing without a signed consent form from the client.
- Per Zillow's Terms of Use, Zillow reviews may not be copied or republished. You may instead link or direct viewers to your Zillow profile from your website, email signature, or social media.

### Red Flag Statements

Avoid statements that may be misleading, deceptive, or inaccurate, such as:

- "Benefits."
- "Best," "lowest," "fastest," or other superlatives.
- "Partner" or "partnership."
- "Credit Edge."
- "Counselor."
- "Preferred."
- "Credit improvement," "credit tips," or "credit tricks."
- "Free credit report/score."
- "Free market analysis."
- "Free food" (acceptable for consumers, not for REALTORS®).
- "Bank," "banking," or "banker."
- "Great retirement tool" (for reverse mortgages).
- "Rebates."
- Alcohol-related terms (beer, wine, champagne, etc.).
- Marijuana-related images or words.
- Spanish-language advertising such as "Hablo Español" without proper review.
- Negative statements.

**Prohibited language by state:**

- Minnesota: cannot use "Banker."
- All states: "lowest," "best," "fastest" — especially regarding rates.
- "Preferred Realtor," "Realtor Partner," "Referral Partner." (Recommended substitutions: "Experienced Realtor," "Professional Realtor," "Experienced Professional.")

### Equal Housing Lender (EHL) vs. Equal Housing Opportunity (EHO)

- EHL focuses on the fair lending of money toward a purchase. Mortgage lenders use the EHL logo on external communications, and lender ads should use the EHL logo.
- EHO focuses on getting people into homes fairly, and is more appropriate for a leasing office or a REALTOR®.

## Licensing and NMLS Disclosures

Each company is responsible for displaying its own NMLS-registered license information for every state in which it is licensed. License numbers and the specific license type (for example, "Mortgage Lender License," "Mortgage Banker License," or "Consumer Loan Company License") vary by state.

When building any disclosure or license listing:

- Use the company's legal name and its company NMLS ID.
- List the correct state license type and number for each licensed state, exactly as recorded on the NMLS Consumer Access site (https://nmlsconsumeraccess.org/).
- Include any state-specific disclosure language required by that state's regulator.
- Confirm current numbers and wording with your company's compliance and licensing department before publishing, as license types and numbers change over time.
